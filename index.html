FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions about our CA services and Indian tax compliance.

Who is required to file an Income Tax Return in India?

Any individual whose gross total income exceeds the basic exemption limit (₹2.5 lakh for below 60 years, ₹3 lakh for senior citizens) must file an ITR. Additionally, individuals with foreign assets, those claiming refunds, or those with income from business, profession, or capital gains must also file. It is advisable for all working individuals to file ITR regardless of liability.

What documents are required for GST Registration?

For GST registration you need: PAN card of the business/proprietor, Aadhaar card, proof of business registration (Certificate of Incorporation / Partnership Deed), address proof of place of business (rent agreement / utility bill), bank account details (cancelled cheque or passbook), and a photograph of the proprietor/directors. Additional documents may be required based on business type.

How do I file GST returns and what are the due dates?

GST returns must be filed on the GST portal (gst.gov.in). The key returns are: GSTR-1 (outward supplies, due 11th of next month), GSTR-3B (monthly summary, due 20th of next month), and GSTR-9 (annual return, due December 31). Businesses under the Composition Scheme file CMP-08 quarterly. Late filing attracts a penalty of ₹50 per day (₹20 for nil returns).

What services does a Chartered Accountant provide?

A Chartered Accountant provides a wide range of services including: statutory and tax audits, income tax and GST return filing, financial statement preparation, company law compliance (ROC filings), business registration services, financial planning and advisory, management consulting, forensic accounting, and representation before tax authorities. CAs are the only professionals legally authorized to conduct company audits in India.

What is the difference between the Old and New Income Tax Regime?

The Old Regime allows deductions under sections like 80C (up to ₹1.5 lakh), 80D (health insurance), HRA, LTA, etc. but has higher base tax rates. The New Regime (default from FY 2023-24) offers lower slab rates but most deductions are not available (except 80CCD(2) for NPS employer contribution). The best regime depends on your income level and available deductions — we help you calculate and choose the more beneficial option.

Is it mandatory for a small business to maintain accounts?

Under Section 44AA of the Income Tax Act, businesses with turnover exceeding ₹25 lakh (or professionals with receipts over ₹10 lakh) must maintain books of accounts. Additionally, if turnover exceeds ₹1 crore (₹10 crore for digital transactions), a tax audit under Section 44AB is mandatory. Under GST, all registered businesses must maintain proper records of invoices, purchases, and stock.
